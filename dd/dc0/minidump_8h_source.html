5 The minidump function wrappers. MiniDumpWriteDump can easily be called
6 to write out a dump of the current process. The only problem is that
7 the dump for the current thread will show that it's coming from deep
8 inside MiniDumpWriteDump. Unfortunately, with DBGHELP.DLL being updated
9 each release of WinDBG, we are missing symbols. That means it's nearly
10 impossible to walk the stack back to your code. These wrappers fix that
11 by spawning a thread to do the write. While not a perfect solution, at
12 least you'll get a cleaner dump. Keep in mind you might have to walk
13 the stack quite a bit to get back to your code, especially if used from
14 something like my SUPERASSERT (v2) dialog.
15 
16 Note that these functions do NO checking if the older broken versions of
17 the mini dump commands are there. The broken versions would hang if
18 called inside the process space. Any version of DBGHELP.DLL from WinDBG
19 3.0.20 or later, or Windows XP will work just fine.
20 
21 ----------------------------------------------------------------------*/
